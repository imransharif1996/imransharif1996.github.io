About

01

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in Computer Science at Kansas State University, advised by Dr. Doina Caragea. My dissertation focuses on transformer-based deep learning for automated rodent social behavior recognition, developing novel multi-stream architectures with cross-stream attention and adaptive fusion mechanisms.

My research spans deep learning, computer vision, multimodal learning, medical imaging, and agricultural AI. I design end-to-end transformer and attention-based architectures for complex visual understanding tasks, with particular expertise in video analysis, multi-stream feature fusion, and federated learning.
