About
I am a fourth-year PhD candidate at Kansas State University, advised by Dr. Doina Caragea. My dissertation focuses on transformer-based deep learning for automated rodent social behavior recognition, developing multi-stream architectures with cross-stream attention and adaptive fusion mechanisms. My broader research spans computer vision, multimodal learning, medical imaging, and agricultural AI.
Research Areas

Computer Vision

Image and video understanding, object detection, segmentation, and visual recognition systems.

Deep Learning

Neural network architectures, representation learning, feature fusion, and model optimization.

Medical Imaging

Clinical image analysis, multimodal classification, distributed learning, and decision support systems.

Agricultural AI

Crop health monitoring, disease detection, pest identification, and precision agriculture systems.
